## User story

As a recipient who just opened a friend's `#/shared` link and spent two minutes reading their eat list, avoid list, and habit stack, I want a single "Adopt this as a starting point" affordance on the shared-view banner that — when I tap it — walks me through consent, drops me into a slightly-modified onboarding where my friend's habit stack and eat list pre-populate the intake's "what are you already doing" field, and then composes my first plan against my own intake answers with my friend's protocol named in the prompt as "the pattern they came in already trying" — so that the trust I just borrowed from my friend converts into a real onboarded user with their own composed protocol, rather than evaporating when I close the tab.

## Why now (four lenses)

### Product Owner

The shareable protocol link (0017) opened the channel. The recipient lands inside Almanac in shared-view mode, reads a real specific protocol, and the banner CTA says "Start your own". Tapping that CTA today does exactly one thing: it clears the shared-view sentinel and routes to `#/welcome` with the consent box unticked. The recipient then types their own profile from scratch. Every byte of their friend's protocol — which they just spent two minutes reading and chose to act on — gets thrown away at the moment of conversion. That is the wrong shape. The smallest meaningful unit of value is a second CTA on the shared-view banner — "Adopt as a starting point" — that holds the shared payload in memory across the consent gate and the onboarding form, surfaces the friend's habit titles and top three eat-list foods as a pre-filled "you mentioned you're already trying" line in the onboarding's free-form `goals` / `conditions` field, and then — when the user runs their first plan compose — passes a single extra line into the existing `formatAdherence()` / `formatIntake()` chain: "This user started after a friend shared their protocol with them. The friend's habit stack centered on: <habit titles>. The friend's eat list centered on: <top three foods>. Treat this as context, not constraint — generate a plan against this user's own intake answers, but acknowledge the pattern they came in trying." No fork. No literal copy. No shared identity. Just the structured carry-over of the conversation that brought them in.

### Stakeholder

This is the **structured artifact** moat working in compound with the **persistent timeline** moat. The share link's job (0017) was to make the friend-to-friend channel exist. The adopt-and-adapt ticket's job is to make that channel *convert*. Every other wellness app's referral surface drops the recipient into a generic onboarding — MyFitnessPal sends them through the same five screens whether they came from a friend or a billboard. Almanac's local-first architecture gives us a structural advantage no backend product can replicate: the friend's protocol bytes are *already on the recipient's device* (they came in via the URL hash), so we can read from them during onboarding without any cross-user data transfer. This widens the moat by turning a one-time read (shared-view rendering) into a *seeded* persistent timeline (the first composed plan acknowledges the seed). It also creates a small editorial artifact at the moment of conversion: the new user's first plan opens with a single line — "You came in after reading <a friend's name redacted>'s protocol. Here is yours." — that no LLM-only wrapper could compose, because no other product has the structural awareness of how the user arrived.

### User (at 7am on the phone)

Sunday morning. My friend sent me a link last night and said "this is what I've been doing." I tapped it. I read their eat list. Three foods I'd already been thinking about; two I hadn't. I tapped their habit stack — five things; one of them is the one I've been failing at for three months. I tap the banner at the top: "Adopt as a starting point". A modal: "We will not copy your friend's labs, insights, or biology — only the pattern of what they were already trying. You will set up your own profile, and your first plan will be built against your own answers." I tap Continue. The consent gate. I tick the box. The onboarding form. The "what are you already trying" field is pre-filled with one line: "A friend's protocol I just read leaned on: morning protein, magnesium glycinate before bed, an afternoon walk." I edit it lightly — I add "but I'm not sleeping" — and continue. I pick my dietary pattern. I paste my API key. I land on `#/plan` with a "Compose from intake" button (the 0007 flow). I tap it. Thirty seconds later my first plan renders with a small editorial header above the snapshot: "You started here after reading a friend's protocol. The pattern of what they were trying is the context; the protocol below is yours."

### Growth

This is the highest-leverage acquisition close we can ship without breaking the privacy contract or widening the egress allow-list. The share link (0017) is the open; the adopt-and-adapt CTA is the close. The acquisition arithmetic compounds across the funnel: a referred recipient who converts is worth ~3-4× a cold visitor by every comparable wellness-product retention curve we can think of, because the social endorsement was the trust transfer; we have not yet capitalized on that endorsement at the conversion moment. (Hypothesis: the adopt-and-adapt CTA lifts shared-view-to-onboarded conversion by 30-60% relative to the bare "Start your own" CTA, because the recipient who already invested two minutes reading the protocol is structurally primed to act on what they read; back-of-envelope from how Notion's "duplicate this page" CTA converts at a multiple of "Sign up" on the same shared template — treat as untested until we have a way to compare without remote analytics.) The second-order growth value is also real: a referred user whose first plan acknowledges the referral becomes a stronger referrer themselves, because their own protocol carries a small piece of editorial provenance ("you started after a friend") that they can point to when their next friend asks.

## Acceptance criteria

Each box maps 1:1 to a Playwright test scenario.

- [ ] The shared-view masthead banner from 0017 gains a second CTA next to "Start your own →". The new CTA reads "Adopt as a starting point". The two CTAs render at equal visual weight (same ink token, same hairline rule) — the existing "Start your own" stays unchanged in label and behavior; the new CTA is additive.
- [ ] Tapping "Adopt as a starting point" opens a small modal with the verbatim copy: "We will not copy your friend's labs, insights, or biology — only the pattern of what they were already trying. You will set up your own profile, and your first plan will be built against your own answers." The modal has two buttons: "Continue" and "Cancel". Cancel closes the modal with zero side effects.
- [ ] Continue does three things in order: (1) writes a structured `AdoptSeed` shape into `localStorage["almanac.adoptSeed"]` — `{ version: 1; habitTitles: string[]; topEatFoods: string[]; capturedAt: number }` with at most five habit titles and at most three eat-list food names extracted from the shared payload by literal field reads (no LLM, no inference); (2) calls `exitSharedView()` so the shared-view sentinel and in-memory payload clear; (3) routes to `#/welcome`. The recipient lands on the welcome page with the consent box unticked and a small inline notice above the consent points: "You came in from a friend's shared protocol. We carried over what they were already trying — nothing else."
- [ ] `AdoptSeed` carries **no** other fields. Asserted by a structural test that constructs a fully-populated shared payload with every field stuffed with sentinel strings (e.g. `plan.snapshot = "__ALMANAC_SENTINEL_SNAPSHOT__"`, `plan.lifestyle[0].title = "__ALMANAC_SENTINEL_LIFESTYLE__"`, `mealPlan.days[0].breakfast.title = "__ALMANAC_SENTINEL_MEAL__"`) and confirms the localStorage seed contains none of the sentinels — only the habit titles and the top three eat-list `food` strings.
- [ ] After consent and through to onboarding, the existing onboarding form's `goals` field surfaces a pre-filled hint line at the top of the textarea (rendered as a small italicized prefix above the textarea's editable area, not inside the textarea — keystroke editing of the user's actual answer never collides with the seed). The prefix reads: "A friend's protocol leaned on: <habit titles joined with ', '> · <top eat-list foods joined with ', '>. Edit below to add your own."
- [ ] The seed persists across browser refresh during the consent + onboarding flow. Asserted by entering shared-view, tapping Adopt, force-reloading the page on `#/welcome`, ticking consent, and confirming the prefix still renders on the onboarding form.
- [ ] On successful onboarding submit (when the user lands on `#/plan` with a saved profile but no plan yet), the seed survives but moves: a new `Profile.adoptSeed?: AdoptSeed` field carries the seed forward, the localStorage entry is cleared (single source of truth). The Plan page's empty state renders the existing 0007 intake-only compose primary CTA, with a single editorial line above the CTA: "You came in after reading a friend's protocol. Your plan will be built against your own intake — the friend's pattern is context only."
- [ ] On compose (the existing 0007 intake-only path, OR the panels-first compose if labs got uploaded between onboarding and compose), `formatIntake()` (or the equivalent intake-summary helper in `src/claude.ts`) appends a single labeled block to the prompt when `profile.adoptSeed` is present: "Referral context (not constraint): the user arrived after reading a friend's protocol that leaned on: <habits>. The friend's eat list emphasized: <foods>. Treat this as context only — build the plan against this user's own intake, but you may acknowledge the pattern in a single line if it lands honestly." The block is gated by the seed; absent seed, the block is absent (no empty line, no trailing whitespace difference — the prompt is byte-identical to the pre-0020 version).
- [ ] The composed plan's `snapshot` field is asserted via fixture to surface one editorial sentence acknowledging the referral when the seed is present. The fixture for this is added under `tests/fixtures/plan.adopt-seed.json` mirroring the existing `plan.json` shape, with a snapshot string that begins "You came in after reading a friend's protocol." — proves the prompt-side wiring carries through to the rendered plan without re-prompting LLM behavior.
- [ ] After the first plan is composed, `profile.adoptSeed` is cleared (the seed is single-use). Subsequent re-composes do not carry it. Asserted by composing a second plan and confirming the second plan's prompt body (via the mock's last-prompt assertion) does NOT contain the "Referral context" block.
- [ ] The "Start your own" CTA on the shared-view banner stays unchanged in behavior: it clears shared-view, routes to `#/welcome`, and does NOT write any AdoptSeed. The two CTAs are genuinely independent paths — a user who wants the clean slate gets the clean slate.
- [ ] Adopt mode renders the same shared-view privacy guarantees through every page until "Continue" is tapped — no Anthropic calls, no IndexedDB reads of the recipient's existing data. Asserted across the full flow (shared-view → modal → Continue → welcome → consent → onboarding submit) via the mock's request count (zero) and a stub on `db.allPanels` / `db.latestPlan` / `db.recentCheckIns` that throws on call (zero throws).
- [ ] If the recipient already has a profile + consent on the device (returning user opens a friend's share link), the Adopt CTA is HIDDEN entirely — the surface is unambiguously a first-time-recipient affordance. The banner shows only "Start your own →" exactly as it does today.
- [ ] Renders on both **chromium and mobile-webkit**. Mobile is the primary channel for shared links; the modal opens at ≥44×44 css px touch targets, and the onboarding prefix wraps cleanly at the mobile-webkit viewport (390 px wide).
- [ ] Schema migration: **yes** — Dexie v7 adds the `adoptSeed?: AdoptSeed` field on `Profile`. The migration is read-side optional; a v6 profile loads as v7 with `adoptSeed === undefined` (no data loss). Migration test asserts a v6 export imports into a v7 build with no profile field loss.
- [ ] Privacy E2E (`tests/e2e/privacy.spec.ts`) still passes — no new hostnames; the seed is local-only; the prompt-side block only fires when the seed is present and routes through the existing BYOK egress.
- [ ] New `tests/e2e/adopt-and-adapt.spec.ts` covers: Adopt CTA appears on the shared-view banner only for first-time-recipient devices, modal copy is verbatim, sentinel-exclusion proves the seed carries only habits + top three foods, seed persists across refresh on welcome and onboarding, onboarding prefix renders, compose with seed surfaces the referral-context prompt block (assertable via `mocks.lastPromptBody()`), composed plan's snapshot fixture lands a referral acknowledgment, second compose does NOT carry the seed, "Start your own" CTA still works unchanged, zero-network across the full flow, returning user does not see the Adopt CTA.

## Out of scope

Explicit anti-goals. The dev agent will not do these even if they seem related.

- A literal "copy the friend's plan into my account" path. The whole point is that the new user's plan is built against *their* intake — the friend's pattern is context, not template. Copying would muddy the persistent-timeline asset and create a "whose protocol is this" footgun.
- Carrying the friend's labs, insights, retest plan, or meal plan into the seed. The privacy contract from 0017 is structural — the share payload doesn't carry labs / insights / profile, so the seed structurally cannot either. Only the habit titles and the top three eat-list food strings travel.
- A "name your friend" field in the seed. The seed is anonymous by construction. The friend is the pattern, not the person. A name field would invite social pressure and ad-hoc identity that the privacy contract is built to avoid.
- A "thank your friend" notification or share-back path. There is no two-way channel; the share link is one-way by design. A thank-back surface would require a backend or the recipient knowing the sender's contact details — neither is in scope.
- Persisting the seed past the first compose. The seed is single-use editorial provenance, not a permanent profile attribute. A user 90 days into their protocol does not need their plan re-prompted with the referral context on every recompose.
- An LLM-narrated "your friend's pattern aligns with your goals" analysis on the welcome page. The pre-compose surface is local-only. The acknowledgment fires once, in the first composed plan's snapshot, in the editorial voice — not in a chat reply on welcome.
- A multi-seed combinator ("you came in from two friends, here's the union of their patterns"). One-friend-at-a-time is the discipline; the most-recent seed wins; opening a second share link before consent overwrites the first seed. Tested by opening two share links in succession.
- Carrying the seed across a profile wipe. `clearAllData()` in Settings clears the seed along with the profile, no exception. The seed is treated as profile data, not browser data.

## Engineering notes

Files / patterns the dev should touch. Be specific enough that the dev doesn't have to re-discover the architecture.

- `src/share/shared-state.ts` — add `extractAdoptSeed(state: SharedProtocolState): AdoptSeed` that pulls at most five habit titles from `state.plan.habitStack.habits` and at most three food names from `state.plan.eatList`, in the order they appear (the friend's curation order is the signal). Pure; no localStorage writes.
- `src/share/adopt-seed.ts` (new) — exports `persistAdoptSeed(seed: AdoptSeed): void`, `readAdoptSeed(): AdoptSeed | null`, `clearAdoptSeed(): void`. The persist function writes to `localStorage["almanac.adoptSeed"]`. The read function returns `null` on missing / malformed / version-mismatch. Mirrors the discipline of `src/share/shared-state.ts` — small surface, no novelty.
- `src/types.ts` — add `AdoptSeed { version: 1; habitTitles: string[]; topEatFoods: string[]; capturedAt: number }`. Extend `Profile` with optional `adoptSeed?: AdoptSeed`. All additive.
- `src/chrome.ts` — the shared-view banner branch already exists from 0017. Extend it: when `isSharedView()` is true AND the recipient has no profile in IndexedDB AND no consent flag, emit the second CTA next to "Start your own →". When either condition is false (returning user with a profile, or user who already acknowledged consent on this device), only the original CTA renders. The "no profile" check uses `getProfile()` with the existing read shim's tour/share bypass — asserted to call IndexedDB exactly once per banner render, not per page.
- `src/main.ts` — when the Adopt CTA's modal Continue handler fires, it routes to `#/welcome`. The welcome page's existing inline-notice slot (the one 0017 added for decode failures) gains a second variant for the "you came in from adopt" notice. Re-uses the same DOM idiom; copy varies.
- `src/pages/welcome.ts` — read `readAdoptSeed()`. When non-null, surface the inline notice above the consent points. The notice copy is verbatim per the acceptance bullets. The seed is NOT cleared here — it survives consent acknowledgment and the onboarding form.
- `src/pages/onboarding.ts` — read `readAdoptSeed()`. When non-null, render the italicized prefix above the `goals` textarea. On submit, move the seed from localStorage onto the saved Profile (`profile.adoptSeed = seed`), then call `clearAdoptSeed()` so the localStorage entry is gone; the seed now lives in the persistent timeline (Dexie).
- `src/pages/plan.ts` — empty-state branch surfaces the "you came in after reading a friend's protocol" line when `profile.adoptSeed` is present, above the existing 0007 intake-compose CTA. On successful compose, after the plan is saved, clear `profile.adoptSeed` via `saveProfile({ ...profile, adoptSeed: undefined })` — single-use discipline.
- `src/claude.ts` — `formatIntake()` (or whichever helper writes the intake block into the plan compose prompt) gains a single optional `seed?: AdoptSeed` parameter. When present, appends the labeled "Referral context (not constraint)" block per the acceptance bullets. The block is the LAST thing in the intake section, separated by a blank line. Voice spec: the block opens with "Referral context (not constraint):" — the parenthetical is load-bearing; it tells the LLM to treat the seed as context, not as a literal eat-list to reproduce.
- `tests/fixtures/plan.adopt-seed.json` (new) — a clone of `plan.json` whose `snapshot` opens with "You came in after reading a friend's protocol." Used by the adopt spec to drive the assertion that the rendered plan surfaces the referral acknowledgment.
- `tests/helpers/flows.ts` — add `enterSharedViewWithAdopt(page, payload)` that mirrors `enterSharedView` but also taps the Adopt CTA after the page renders. Add `seedAdoptInLocalStorage(page, seed)` for direct unit-test seeding (skipping the share-view round-trip).
