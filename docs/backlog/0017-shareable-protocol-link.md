## User story

As a user three weeks into a composed protocol whose friend just asked "wait, what is that app you keep talking about," I want a "Share my protocol" affordance on the Plan page that bundles my eat list, avoid list, habit stack, and meal plan — explicitly without my name, my API key, my labs, my insights, or my goals — into a single long URL I can text or post, so that when the friend taps it on their phone they land inside Almanac looking at the real shape of *my* protocol against a sample-tour banner, instead of having to take my word for what the artifact looks like.

## Why now (four lenses)

### Product Owner

Two acquisition wedges have shipped: the marker hero share card (0011) lets a user post a single biomarker delta tile, and the sample tour (0014) lets a prospect navigate a canned fictional Almanac before consenting. There is one acquisition shape neither covers: *a friend's actual protocol, on the friend's recommendation, viewable by the recipient on their own device, without a backend in the middle*. Today the path is "screenshot the eat list, paste into iMessage" — which strips the structure, drops the typography, and reads as a list of foods instead of as a protocol. The smallest meaningful unit of value is a single button on `#/plan` ("Share this protocol") that base64url-encodes a curated subset of the active `Plan` + `MealPlan` into a URL hash payload, opens the OS share sheet with the link, and — when a recipient opens that link — branches the existing 0014 tour code path to render against the *payload* state instead of the canned fixture. No backend, no analytics, no key handoff. The bytes travel inside the link.

### Stakeholder

This widens the **structured artifact** moat into the channel that matters most for organic growth: the friend-to-friend text. Every other wellness app's "share" is a backend-mediated affair — Whoop posts to a Whoop-hosted page, MyFitnessPal builds a server-rendered share URL, Oura's share URLs all 302 through an oura.com domain that can be analytics-tagged and deprecated. Almanac's local-first architecture lets us ship a share artifact that is *literally the bytes of the protocol* encoded in the URL — no server, no tracking, no deprecation risk. That is a category-defining capability we already have; we are just surfacing it. It also compounds 0014: the tour fixture's job becomes "this is what a generic Almanac looks like"; the share link's job is "this is what *my friend's* Almanac looks like." The recipient lands on a real, specific protocol they want to read, with the "Start your own" banner that 0014 already wired. We are not building a second tour surface; we are reusing the one we shipped against a second state source.

### User (at 7am on the phone)

I'm on `#/plan`. I tap the small "Share this protocol" link in the page footer next to "Print or share." A short modal opens: "This will create a link that contains your eat list, avoid list, habits, and meal plan. It will not contain your name, your labs, your goals, your API key, or any insights about your biology. The link works on any phone that opens it." I tap Continue. The OS share sheet opens with a long URL ending in `#/shared?p=...`. I AirDrop it to a friend. They tap it. They land inside Almanac in tour-mode, looking at *my* eat list, *my* habit stack, *my* week of meals — with a banner across the top: "You're reading a protocol shared with you. Nothing here is yours yet. Start your own →". The friend swipes Today → Plan → Meals. Each page is populated from the link's bytes. No insights are visible (I didn't share those). No name is on it (I didn't share that either). They tap "Start your own." The link state clears. They land on `#/welcome` with the consent box waiting to be ticked.

### Growth

This is the highest-leverage acquisition lever we can ship without violating the privacy contract or widening the egress allow-list. The hero share card (0011) drives discovery through public posts; the sample tour (0014) lowers the trust cost of trying the app for a prospect who clicks a link. The shareable protocol link closes the loop between them: a real user's real protocol, sent over a real social channel, viewable on the recipient's phone as a real Almanac surface. (Hypothesis: a link whose payload is the sender's actual protocol converts visit-to-onboarded at roughly 2× the rate of the generic sample tour link, because the recipient is reading something specific and endorsed by someone they trust; back-of-envelope from comparable patterns in Notion's "duplicate this page" share-link mechanic — treat as untested until we have a way to compare without remote analytics.) The link's growth value also compounds with every future Plan-shaped feature: when the rule engine adds a new editorial flourish or the meal plan grows a new layout, the link inherits the upgrade for free.

## Acceptance criteria

Each box maps 1:1 to a Playwright test scenario.

- [ ] `src/share/protocol-link.ts` (new) exports `encodeProtocolPayload(plan: Plan, mealPlan: MealPlan | undefined): string` and `decodeProtocolPayload(encoded: string): SharedProtocolState | null`. The payload schema (`SharedProtocolPayload`) carries exactly: `version: 1`, `eatList`, `avoidList`, `habitStack` (label + habits, no provenance), `mealPlan?` (days + grocery list only, no goals). The encoder JSON-stringifies the payload, gzips via `CompressionStream("gzip")`, then base64url-encodes the bytes. The decoder reverses the chain; on any structural mismatch or version skew it returns `null`.
- [ ] The payload schema explicitly **excludes**: `Profile` (name, sex, dietary pattern, API key, goals, conditions, household), `Panel[]`, `Insight[]` (the full insights array — including provenance), `retest`, `CheckIn[]`, `ProjectionSnapshot[]`. Asserted by a structural test that constructs a fully-populated `Plan` with each excluded field set to a sentinel string and confirms the decoded payload contains none of those sentinels.
- [ ] `#/plan` gains a "Share this protocol" link in the page footer next to the existing "Print or share" link. The link opens a small confirmation modal listing what the shared link will and will not contain (verbatim copy in the engineering notes). The modal has two buttons: "Continue" and "Cancel." Cancel closes the modal with no side effects.
- [ ] Continue calls `encodeProtocolPayload`, constructs a full URL (`location.origin + "/#/shared?p=" + encoded`), and invokes the OS share sheet via `navigator.share({ url })` when `navigator.canShare({ url })` returns true; otherwise it copies the URL to the clipboard via `navigator.clipboard.writeText(url)` and shows a one-line confirmation ("Link copied. Paste it anywhere.").
- [ ] A new route `#/shared` reads the `p=` query param, calls `decodeProtocolPayload`, and — on success — sets a sentinel flag `localStorage["almanac.sharedView"] = "true"` along with an in-memory `SharedProtocolState` and routes to `#/today`. On decode failure, it routes back to `#/welcome` with a one-line inline notice ("That shared link did not decode. Ask your friend to send it again.").
- [ ] When `almanac.sharedView === "true"`, the router branches the same way the sample tour branches (per 0014): it bypasses the consent and profile gates and renders every page against the shared payload. Today, Plan, and Meals render the payload's eatList / avoidList / habitStack / mealPlan. Progress, Labs, Compare, and Recap show editorial empty states ("This was not shared with you.") rather than the host's real data — the shared view never reads from the recipient's IndexedDB and never reads the host fixture from 0014.
- [ ] A persistent banner renders above the masthead on every page in shared-view mode: "You are reading a protocol shared with you. Nothing here is yours yet. Start your own →". The CTA clears `almanac.sharedView`, clears the in-memory state, and routes to `#/welcome` with the consent checkbox unticked. The banner styling re-uses the existing `.tour-banner` token from 0014 — restraint over novelty.
- [ ] Shared-view mode fires **zero Anthropic calls** across a full navigation sweep (`#/today` → `#/plan` → `#/meals` → tap "Start your own"). Asserted against the mock's request count.
- [ ] Shared-view mode reads NO data from IndexedDB. Asserted by stubbing `db.getProfile`, `db.allPanels`, `db.latestPlan`, `db.latestMealPlan`, `db.recentCheckIns` to throw on call, and confirming no throw bubbles through any shared-view render path.
- [ ] The privacy modal copy is verbatim: "This will create a link that contains your eat list, avoid list, habits, and meal plan. It will not contain your name, your labs, your goals, your API key, or any insights about your biology. The link works on any phone that opens it." Tested via DOM string assertion to catch copy drift.
- [ ] When the encoded payload exceeds 8000 characters (a conservative URL limit for iMessage / Twitter / WhatsApp), the modal shows a secondary line: "Your protocol is longer than most messaging apps allow in one link. Tap to copy instead of share." The share button changes label to "Copy long link." Edge case asserted by inflating the meal plan with synthetic days.
- [ ] When the host's `Plan` has no `MealPlan` yet, the share link omits the meal plan from the payload and the recipient's Meals page shows the "This was not shared with you" empty state for that surface alone. Plan + Today still render.
- [ ] The privacy E2E (`tests/e2e/privacy.spec.ts`) still passes — egress allow-list unchanged. Specifically: the share button does not fire any network request, and the `#/shared` route does not fire any network request to anything other than the SPA's own origin.
- [ ] Renders on both **chromium and mobile-webkit**. Mobile is the primary channel for this feature (texted links open on phones); mobile-webkit asserts the modal opens, the share button is tappable (≥44×44 css px), the `navigator.canShare({ url })` code path is exercised, and the recipient flow ends on a populated Today.
- [ ] New `tests/e2e/protocol-link.spec.ts` covers: button appears on Plan, modal copy is verbatim, Continue produces a URL with a non-empty `p=` payload, decoding round-trips the eatList / avoidList / habitStack / mealPlan, decoding does NOT round-trip name / labs / insights / API key (sentinel test), `#/shared` route renders the shared protocol, IndexedDB stubs are never called in shared-view mode, banner CTA clears the state and lands on `#/welcome`, long-payload branch surfaces the "Copy long link" label, malformed payload routes to welcome with the inline notice.

## Out of scope

Explicit anti-goals. The dev agent will not do these even if they seem related.

- A backend-hosted share page. The whole point is that the bytes live in the URL, and the URL works on any phone that opens the SPA. Hosting a `/shared/abc123` page that fetches a payload from a server would widen the egress allow-list, add a privacy disclosure surface, and break the "no backend" non-negotiable.
- An "include my insights" toggle on the share modal. Insights are the most personal claim Almanac makes about the user; they include marker values via the provenance chain (per 0013). Sharing them by default would be a privacy regression; sharing them via opt-in would create a footgun. Insights stay out, period.
- An "include my labs" toggle. Same reasoning. Labs are the data the privacy contract is most stringent about. They never travel through a share link.
- A "shorten this URL" integration (bit.ly, etc.). Any URL shortener is a third-party hostname that would either widen the egress allow-list or proxy through a service we don't control. The "Copy long link" branch handles the long-URL edge case without a third party.
- Persisting received shared protocols on the recipient's device. The shared state is in-memory only — exactly the same discipline as the tour fixture in 0014. Persistence would mean writing someone else's protocol into the recipient's IndexedDB, which muddies whose data is whose.
- A "save this shared protocol as my starting point" button. The two states stay quarantined. The recipient can read it, then start their own; the import path is consent → onboard, not "absorb my friend's settings."
- Linking the share to the marker hero card (0011). Different artifact, different channel. The marker card is for the social feed; the protocol link is for the private text thread. Mixing them produces a less-useful generalization.
- A "share this protocol via QR code" surface. The URL is already the artifact. A QR code is a desktop affordance for an explicitly-mobile-first channel.
- Generating a preview image (Open Graph / iMessage rich preview) for the shared URL. That would require widening egress to a service that renders the preview, or a backend that produces the image — both contract violations.

## Engineering notes

Files / patterns the dev should touch. Be specific enough that the dev doesn't have to re-discover the architecture.

- `src/share/protocol-link.ts` (new) — the encoder/decoder pair plus the `SharedProtocolPayload` interface. The encoder uses `CompressionStream("gzip")` (universally available in modern browsers we target; Vite ships no polyfill, none is needed). The base64url encoding follows the standard alphabet (`-` and `_` substituting `+` and `/`, no padding). Round-trip is asserted in the spec; structural exclusion is asserted via sentinel strings on excluded fields.
- `src/types.ts` — add `SharedProtocolPayload` and `SharedProtocolState` interfaces. The state shape is the decoded payload plus a synthetic empty `Profile` shell the page code can read against without crashing (the shell has empty strings for name/ownerName, an empty API key, and the host's dietary pattern only if it was carried in the payload — for v1 the dietary pattern is NOT carried, keeping the payload schema minimally invasive). All additive.
- `src/share/shared-state.ts` (new) — mirrors `src/sample/state.ts` from 0014. Exposes `isSharedView()`, `enterSharedView(state)`, `exitSharedView()`, and getter shims `sharedPlan()`, `sharedMealPlan()`. The in-memory state is held in a module-scoped variable; `enterSharedView` sets the localStorage flag AND the in-memory state in one call. `exitSharedView` clears both.
- `src/db.ts` — extend the existing 0014 `isTour()`-aware read shims to also check `isSharedView()`. The pattern is: if shared-view, return shared getter; else if tour, return tour getter; else return real IndexedDB. Write paths in shared-view mode surface the same `surfaceInlineTourNotice()` helper (re-used from 0014) with copy adjusted to "This is a shared protocol. Start your own to write data." — one new branch in the helper, keyed on `isSharedView()`.
- `src/main.ts` — router branch: when `isSharedView()` is true and the route is not `#/welcome`, bypass consent + profile gates. Routing TO `#/welcome` always calls `exitSharedView()`. The `#/shared` route handler lives here too; it decodes the payload, calls `enterSharedView(state)`, and `location.hash = "#/today"`. On decode failure, surface the inline notice on `#/welcome`.
- `src/pages/plan.ts` — add the "Share this protocol" link in the existing page footer next to "Print or share." Wire it to a new `openProtocolShareModal()` helper that renders the confirmation modal via the existing `openSlideover` primitive (re-use what 0013 used for the provenance slideover — the modal is the same DOM idiom).
- `src/pages/today.ts` / `src/pages/meals.ts` — at the top of the render function, branch on `isSharedView()` and render the shared payload's data. Pages outside the share scope (Progress, Labs, Compare, Recap) render the editorial empty state described in the acceptance criteria.
- `src/chrome.ts` — `masthead()` already emits the tour banner when `isTour()` is true (from 0014). Extend it: when `isSharedView()` is true, emit the same `.tour-banner` block with the shared-view copy. The CTA on click calls `exitSharedView()` rather than `exitTour()`. Keep the styling token shared — one CSS class, two semantic uses.
- `src/styles.css` — re-use `.tour-banner` from 0014. No new colors. The modal can borrow from the existing `.modal` / `.slideover` styles already on the Plan page; no new layout primitives.
- `tests/helpers/flows.ts` — add `enterSharedView(page, payload)` and `exitSharedView(page)` helpers. The `enterSharedView` helper navigates to `#/shared?p=<encoded>` so the spec exercises the real route handler, not a backdoor.
- `tests/e2e/protocol-link.spec.ts` (new) — every acceptance bullet maps to a `test()` or `expect()`. Re-use `composePlan` to seed a real plan, then drive the share modal. The sentinel-exclusion test inflates a Plan with named sentinels (e.g. `plan.insights[0].title = "__ALMANAC_SENTINEL_INSIGHT__"`) and confirms the encoded payload does not contain the sentinel.
- `tests/e2e/privacy.spec.ts` — extend with a "shared-view traversal" sweep mirroring the tour sweep from 0014. Visits `#/shared?p=<payload>` and every shared-mode-rendered page, confirms no off-allow-list egress.
- Schema migration: **no**. The shared payload is in-memory + URL only; no IndexedDB writes.
- Egress allow-list change: **no**. The link is the artifact; there is no remote.
- New deps: **no**. `CompressionStream` and `navigator.share` / `navigator.clipboard` are platform primitives.
- Voice spec for the modal + banner copy: the modal copy is the verbatim privacy disclosure quoted in the acceptance criteria. The banner copy mirrors the tour banner ("You are reading a protocol shared with you. Nothing here is yours yet. Start your own →"). The inline decode-failure notice: "That shared link did not decode. Ask your friend to send it again." No exclamation, no promise language.
