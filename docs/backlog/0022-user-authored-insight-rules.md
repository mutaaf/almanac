## User story

As a precision-health-literate user who has read the GLUT4 / fasting-insulin literature and disagrees with one of our curated rules, OR who wants to encode a multi-marker pattern we did not ship (e.g. "homocysteine above 9 µmol/L AND B12 below 500 pg/mL fires methylation-cofactor-undermet"), I want a Settings → Insight rules page where I can author a new rule with a name, a category, an evaluation expression over markers (one-or-more `marker comparator threshold` clauses combined with AND/OR), a one-paragraph detail, a priority, and a citation URL or DOI — saved locally, evaluated alongside the curated `RULES[]` on every plan compose, shown in the provenance slideover (0013) with a "user-authored" badge and the citation link — so that the insight engine becomes mine, not just ours, and the rules I'm willing to defend in writing become part of my protocol's reasoning.

## Why now (four lenses)

### Product Owner

The user-extensible marker database (0002) was the first move on this axis: we ship a curated seed, we let the user extend it locally, the LLM honors the extension as authoritative, and the share / export / import flows round-trip the extension. The same logic applies one layer up. We ship 18 curated rules in `src/insights.ts`. The literature has hundreds. We cannot — and should not — try to curate the long tail; what we should do is ship a strong seed engine and let the technically-literate user extend it. The smallest meaningful unit of value is a single Settings page (`#/settings` gains a "Your insight rules" subsection) where the user can author a rule with: a stable id, a human-readable title, a category (pattern | trend), a list of `RuleClause` predicates (`{ markerKey, comparator, threshold }`), a combinator (all / any — AND / OR), a detail paragraph, a priority (high | medium | low), and a citation field (URL, DOI, or free-form). On save, the rule is persisted to a new `userRules` Dexie table; on every subsequent `computeInsights()` call, the user rules are evaluated alongside the curated `RULES[]` array, with user-authored insights tagged so they render with a small "user-authored" badge in the provenance slideover (0013) and a citation link. The discipline from 0002 carries through: user entries do NOT mutate the curated seed, and the citation field is mandatory — every user rule must declare its source, on the editorial bar that we already hold for the curated seed.

### Stakeholder

This is the **programmatic insight engine** moat extended along the same axis 0002 extended the marker DB. The engine has been load-bearing since v1, auditable since 0013, interactive since 0019 (once shipped); the next state is *extensible*. A backend-shaped competitor could ship a "rule editor" but would have to evaluate every user's rules on a shared infrastructure, which (a) creates a per-user-compute economic problem, (b) requires the user to ship their labs to the server for the rules to evaluate against, and (c) inherits liability for any user-authored rule that produces a clinically-risky output. Almanac's local-first architecture sidesteps all three: the user's rules evaluate entirely on-device against their own labs, the curated `RULES[]` array stays unchanged (no contamination risk for other users), and the liability surface is symmetric to the existing user-defined-marker risk surface (which we already accept). It also opens a quiet acquisition wedge: a single user who authors a thoughtful rule with a citation and posts a screenshot to the precision-health forum is the highest-density editorial demonstration of "this engine is serious" we can produce — strictly stronger than the provenance slideover (0013) on a curated rule, because the user-authored version proves the engine is extensible by people who are not us.

### User (at 7am on the phone)

Saturday morning, on the couch with the laptop. I am reading a recent paper on homocysteine and B12. I want my engine to know what I know. I go to `#/settings`. I scroll to "Your insight rules" — a new subsection between profile and export/import. I tap "Author a rule." A small form: rule title ("Methylation cofactor undermet"), category (pattern), clause 1 (homocysteine > 9 µmol/L), clause 2 (B12 < 500 pg/mL), combinator (all), detail paragraph (I type a sentence about MTR / MTRR pathway), priority (medium), citation (I paste a DOI). I tap Save. The rule appears in the list. I tap "Recompose plan" from the Plan page. The new plan fires my rule against my latest panel. Two days later, on the Plan page, I tap the new insight card. The provenance slideover (0013) opens, shows the rule's clauses with my values + thresholds, and a small footer reads "User-authored. Cited: <DOI>." I tap the citation. The browser opens it in a new tab. My friend who lent me the paper texts me: "you put that in your app?" I screenshot the slideover and send it back.

### Growth

This is the moat-deepening ticket of the four — the audience is the technically-literate user already deep inside the app, not a fresh prospect. But it produces an unusually high-density editorial artifact at the convergence of (1) the user's expertise, (2) the citation discipline (which makes the rule defensible in writing), and (3) the editorial voice of the provenance slideover. (Hypothesis: a user-authored rule with a citation, screenshotted from the provenance slideover, lands on a precision-health forum with ~3-5× the engagement of a curated-rule provenance screenshot, because the social proof is "this user understood the literature well enough to encode it" not "this app encoded the literature" — the same engagement multiplier that user-authored Excalidraw libraries enjoy over the bundled shapes; back-of-envelope from comparable patterns in Roam Research's user-shared queries — treat as untested.) The second-order growth value: every user who authors a rule is a higher-LTV retention case by construction, because they now have a sunk-cost commitment to the engine that runs them — and the engine that runs only Almanac.

## Acceptance criteria

Each box maps 1:1 to a Playwright test scenario.

- [ ] `src/insights/user-rules.ts` (new) exports `evaluateUserRule(rule: UserRule, ctx: RuleContext): Omit<PreComputedInsight, "id" | "category"> | null`. The function reads the rule's clauses, evaluates each clause against `ctx.latest(clause.markerKey)`, combines via the rule's `combinator`, and — when the rule fires — returns the same shape the curated `RULES[]` already returns, with `supportingMarkers` populated from every clause whose marker had a value and `evidence` built from the same templates ("X = N unit"). The function is pure; no DOM, no Dexie.
- [ ] `UserRule` shape (in `src/types.ts`): `{ id: string; title: string; category: "pattern" | "trend"; clauses: RuleClause[]; combinator: "all" | "any"; detail: string; priority: "high" | "medium" | "low"; citation: string; createdAt: number }`. `RuleClause` shape: `{ markerKey: string; comparator: "<" | "<=" | "=" | ">=" | ">"; threshold: number }`. `combinator: "all"` is AND; `combinator: "any"` is OR. `clauses` is non-empty (validation enforces ≥ 1).
- [ ] `computeInsights()` in `src/insights.ts` is extended to load `listUserRules()` from the new `userRules` Dexie table and run each user rule through `evaluateUserRule()` after the curated `RULES[]` and `TREND_RULES[]` have been evaluated. User rules append to the result; the curated rules are evaluated FIRST so a curated rule's id can never collide with a user rule's id (user rules are id-prefixed `"user:"` by construction). The returned `PreComputedInsight[]` order is curated-first, then user, then trend.
- [ ] User-authored insights carry a `provenance.userAuthored: true` flag and `provenance.citation: string` field. The Plan-page provenance slideover (0013) renders the user-authored badge ("User-authored.") and a "Cited:" line with the citation rendered as either a clickable anchor (when the citation is a valid HTTP/HTTPS URL) or as plain text (when it's a DOI or free-form string). The badge styling re-uses the existing `.insight__provenance-rule-meta` token from 0013 — no new colors.
- [ ] When the citation is a clickable URL, the anchor opens in a new tab with `rel="noopener noreferrer"` AND `target="_blank"`. The privacy E2E asserts that the anchor's href is NOT auto-fetched by the page (no prefetch, no preconnect) — opening a citation is a user-initiated action, not a render-side network call.
- [ ] Settings → "Your insight rules" subsection lists all user rules, sorted by `createdAt` descending. Each row shows the rule's title, the combinator + clause count ("3 clauses, all"), the priority, and a "Delete" action. Deletion is a confirm-then-remove flow (one modal) and is irreversible — exactly the pattern 0002 uses for user markers. The list also has an "Author a rule" CTA at the top that opens the rule-author form.
- [ ] The rule-author form validates: title is non-empty and ≤ 80 chars; at least one clause is present; every clause's `markerKey` resolves via `findMarker()` (the marker must exist — built-in OR user-defined per 0002); every clause's threshold is a finite number; the citation field is non-empty (the editorial bar is that every user rule declares its source). Validation failures surface inline next to the offending field; the form's Save button is disabled until validation passes.
- [ ] On Save, the rule is persisted to the `userRules` Dexie table with a generated id (`"user:<uuid>"`). The form closes, the list refreshes, and the user sees the new rule. The next plan compose (or `computeInsights()` call, whether or not the user recomposes) evaluates the new rule.
- [ ] User rules participate in the Plan compose prompt: the existing prompt block that lists deterministic findings now includes user-authored ones, with a small "(user-authored)" suffix per finding. The LLM is instructed in `PLAN_VOICE` to treat user-authored findings the same way it treats curated findings — as authoritative — and to reference them in the snapshot if they materially shape the plan. The voice spec note in `PLAN_VOICE` is one sentence: "Treat user-authored findings as authoritative; the user has cited their source."
- [ ] Export (`#/settings` Export JSON) round-trips user rules — the exported JSON carries a `userRules: UserRule[]` field. Import accepts user rules and merges them into the local Dexie table (`upsertUserRules`), preserving `createdAt` from the import. Round-trip is asserted by export → wipe → import → re-render with the rule list intact.
- [ ] User rules are evaluated in **deterministic order** within their category: by `createdAt` ascending. Two rules that fire on the same panel surface in createdAt order so the user can reason about precedence.
- [ ] The what-if simulator (0019, once shipped) honors user rules — a user dragging a marker on the simulator sees user-authored insights toggle alongside curated ones. The "would newly fire" badge applies to user rules; the citation renders in the slideover. Asserted by a spec that seeds a user rule, enters the simulator, and confirms the rule's diff state.
- [ ] User rules NEVER write to `Plan.insights[]` outside the regular `computeInsights()` path; the plan compose call is the only thing that persists insights to the plan. A user-authored rule that fires shows up in the plan's insights with `provenance.userAuthored === true` and the citation field carried through.
- [ ] Privacy E2E (`tests/e2e/privacy.spec.ts`) still passes — no new hostnames; user rules are local-only; citation URLs are rendered as anchors but never auto-fetched.
- [ ] Schema migration: **yes** — Dexie v(N+1) adds the `userRules` table with shape `&id, createdAt`. Migration test asserts a v(N) export imports into v(N+1) with no data loss; new table starts empty for migrated users.
- [ ] Tour mode (0014) does NOT render the user-rules UI — the Settings page is gated behind consent and a real profile; tour mode bypasses Settings entirely as it does today. Shared-view mode (0017) does not render Settings either.
- [ ] New `tests/e2e/user-insight-rules.spec.ts` covers: list renders empty initially, form validates each field, save persists and refreshes the list, evaluate fires the rule against a seeded panel, provenance slideover renders the user-authored badge + citation, citation URL renders as a clickable anchor with noopener/noreferrer, citation DOI renders as plain text, delete removes the rule and removes its insight on next compose, export round-trips the rules, prompt body includes the user-authored finding (assertable via `mocks.lastPromptBody()`), the simulator (0019) honors user rules in the diff.

## Out of scope

Explicit anti-goals. The dev agent will not do these even if they seem related.

- A "community catalog" of user-authored rules. Cross-user sharing requires a backend or a curated submission process; neither is in scope. Users can export their rules and share JSON files manually if they want to seed a friend's instance.
- A sex- or age-conditional clause shape. The MVP comparator is `marker comparator threshold`. Sex / age conditioning is a future ticket if real users want it; the curated `RULES[]` array can demonstrate the pattern (e.g. ferritin floor varies by sex) without the user form needing to model it.
- A "lab-range-relative" comparator (e.g. "below 60% of the optimal range floor"). MVP is absolute numeric thresholds. Range-relative comparisons are richer but invite ambiguity at authoring time.
- A free-form JavaScript / expression evaluator. The clause shape is deliberately constrained to `{ markerKey, comparator, threshold }` AND/OR'd by `combinator: "all" | "any"`. A free-form expression evaluator would invite injection-shaped footguns and would couple the engine to a parser we'd then have to maintain. If a user needs more expressiveness, the answer is to ship a richer clause shape in a future ticket — not to open an arbitrary-expression door.
- A trend-rule authoring path. MVP is pattern rules (single-panel). Trend rules (multi-panel time series) require the user to express temporal logic, which is a significantly more complex form. The category `"trend"` is part of the schema so a future ticket can extend the form without a migration; v1 surfaces "pattern" only in the author UI.
- An LLM-assisted "draft a rule for me" affordance. The whole point is that user-authored rules carry the user's reasoning and citation. An LLM drafting the rule would dilute the citation discipline and re-introduce the "AI said so" perception we are trying to escape.
- Mutating the curated `RULES[]` array. Curated rules are immutable from the UI. A user who disagrees with a curated rule authors a new rule of their own; if the curated rule needs revision, that is a ticket against `src/insights.ts` proper.
- A "test this rule against my panel" preview before saving. Future-ticket worthwhile; out of scope for the MVP form because adding a live preview doubles the form's surface and risks the user authoring a rule against the wrong intuition. The "save then re-render" loop is fast enough that the preview is the next render.
- Carrying user-authored rules into the shared protocol link (0017). The share link's privacy contract is that no inference about the user's biology travels; an insight rule is inference. User rules stay strictly local.

## Engineering notes

Files / patterns the dev should touch. Be specific enough that the dev doesn't have to re-discover the architecture.

- `src/insights/user-rules.ts` (new) — `evaluateUserRule(rule, ctx)`, `listUserRules()`, `addUserRule()`, `deleteUserRule()`, `upsertUserRules(arr)`. The evaluator follows the existing `RULES[]` Rule interface so a user rule plugs into `computeInsights()` with the same `Omit<PreComputedInsight, "id" | "category">` shape. The id is prefixed `"user:"` to guarantee non-collision with curated ids.
- `src/insights.ts` — `computeInsights()` loads user rules via `listUserRules()` and evaluates them after the curated `RULES[]`. The hydrated `provenance` for a user-authored insight gets `userAuthored: true` and `citation` populated. The existing `RULE_GLOSSES` lookup is bypassed for user rules — the gloss comes from the user's `detail` field directly.
- `src/types.ts` — add `UserRule`, `RuleClause`, `RuleComparator`. Extend `InsightProvenance` with optional `userAuthored?: boolean` and optional `citation?: string`. All additive.
- `src/db.ts` — add the `userRules` Dexie table at the next version bump. The table schema is `&id, createdAt`. Helpers: `listUserRules()`, `addUserRule()`, `deleteUserRule(id)`, `upsertUserRules(arr)`. Mirrors the 0002 `userMarkers` plumbing exactly.
- `src/pages/settings.ts` — add the "Your insight rules" subsection. The author form is a slideover (re-uses the existing `openSlideover` primitive from 0013); the list is a simple per-row layout with delete actions. Form validation is inline; the Save button is disabled until valid.
- `src/pages/plan.ts` — no changes beyond the existing provenance slideover wiring. The slideover already reads `insight.provenance` and renders supporting-marker rows; the new branch is a small "user-authored" badge + citation row at the bottom of the slideover when `provenance.userAuthored === true`.
- `src/claude.ts` — the prompt builder that lists deterministic findings extends to suffix user-authored findings with "(user-authored)". `PLAN_VOICE` gains the one-sentence instruction described in the acceptance bullets.
- `src/styles.css` — `.user-rule-form` (slideover form layout), `.user-rule-row` (list row with delete action), `.insight__provenance-user-authored` (the small "User-authored. Cited: …" footer in the slideover — re-uses existing token sizes; oxblood is reserved, ink-soft is the right token for this).
- `src/insights/simulate.ts` (created by 0019) — when 0019 ships, the simulator reads user rules through the same `computeInsights()` path; no changes needed in the simulator module itself. The integration assertion in 0019's spec gains a new test for user-rule diff behavior, but that lives in this ticket's spec, not 0019's.
- `tests/helpers/flows.ts` — add `seedUserRule(page, rule: UserRule)` and `clearUserRules(page)` helpers via `page.evaluate` against Dexie.
- `tests/e2e/user-insight-rules.spec.ts` (new) — every acceptance bullet maps to a test. The "evaluate against seeded panel" test seeds a panel with ferritin = 25 ng/mL and B12 = 350 pg/mL, seeds a user rule with `(ferritin < 30) AND (B12 < 500)` combinator `"all"`, recomposes the plan (via the mock), asserts the user rule fires and surfaces in `plan.insights[]` with the citation.
- `tests/e2e/privacy.spec.ts` — extend with a "user-rule citation does not auto-fetch" assertion: seed a rule with a citation pointing to `https://example.com/citation`, render the slideover, and confirm `example.com` is NOT in the captured network log. The citation is a render-side anchor only; activation is user-initiated.
- Schema migration: **yes** — Dexie v(N+1). The migration mirrors the 0002 user-markers v5 migration.
- Egress allow-list change: **no**. Citations are rendered as anchors; they do not auto-fetch.
- New deps: **no**. UUID generation uses `crypto.randomUUID()` (universally available).
- Voice spec: the form labels are restrained ("Title," "Category," "Clauses," "When all/any clause fires," "Detail," "Priority," "Citation"). The slideover footer reads "User-authored. Cited:" followed by the citation. The list-row priority pill re-uses existing `.priority-high` / `.priority-medium` / `.priority-low` tokens. Banned: "amazing," "exciting," "journey," "great." No exclamation marks anywhere in the form copy.
