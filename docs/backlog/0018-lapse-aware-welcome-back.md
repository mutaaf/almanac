## User story

As a user who composed a protocol six weeks ago, used it for ten days, then went quiet for a month and just reopened the app on a Tuesday morning, I want the first thing I see to be a single editorial "Welcome back" surface — naming exactly how long it's been, the projection windows that opened while I was away, the habits whose cadence quietly broke, the marker retest that is now overdue or coming due — instead of the regular Today screen which silently treats me as if I never left, so that the lapse becomes legible to me, the catch-up has a one-tap shape, and I have a reason to pick the protocol back up rather than uninstall the app.

## Why now (four lenses)

### Product Owner

We have shipped four cadence surfaces — Today (daily), Quiet-day card (between-cadences daily, ticket 0015), Sunday recap (weekly, ticket 0008), Next-draw projection (between-draws on Progress, ticket 0012). Each surface assumes the user is *engaged*. None of them handle the most common shape of a real precision-health user's behaviour: a high-intensity opening burst, then a long quiet, then a tentative reopen. Today, a lapsed user who comes back sees the same Today screen they saw on day three — three meal cards, a habit stack, a streak strip that says "0 of 7 days" because they haven't checked in, and the quiet-day card (which assumes recent check-ins exist to compute a precedence). The screen reads as accusatory rather than welcoming, and the user closes the app. The smallest meaningful unit of value is one new surface — `#/welcome-back` — that fires *only* when the gap between the user's previous-session timestamp and the current open exceeds a threshold (14 days), renders a single page of "here is what happened while you were away," and ends with two equally-weighted CTAs: "Pick up where I left off" (route to Today) and "Re-compose with the time off counted as honest data" (route to the Plan recompose flow with adherence pre-populated as honest skips). No LLM call. No new schema beyond a `lastSessionAt` timestamp that the app already half-tracks via the existing check-in rows but doesn't read for routing.

### Stakeholder

This is the **adherence loop** moat extended into the most consequential surface in the funnel that today does not exist: the lapse-recovery surface. The adherence loop has been load-bearing for plan composition (since v1), the Sunday recap (since 0008), and the quiet-day card (since 0015). All of those assume continuity. The lapse-aware welcome-back surface is the first surface to assume *discontinuity*, and it is exactly the surface a backend-shaped product cannot ship without making it creepy — a server-side product would email a "we miss you" reactivation note, which is the anti-pattern. The local-first version is fundamentally different: the surface only exists if the user themselves reopens the app, and it is constructed entirely from local state. It widens the moat in two ways: (1) it deepens the persistent-timeline asset by making the timeline *legible* across a gap rather than hiding the gap, and (2) it deepens the structured-artifact moat by turning a re-engagement moment into another editorial artifact — "Welcome back. The last 34 days. Your magnesium habit hasn't been logged since April 7. Your ferritin projection window opened on April 22 and is still open. Your retest was scheduled for May 12; tap to push the date." The same editorial surface every other cadence has, applied to the most fragile point in the user's relationship with the product.

### User (at 7am on the phone)

Tuesday morning. I last opened Almanac on April 7. It's May 19. I tap the icon. Instead of Today, a single editorial page renders: "Welcome back. It has been 42 days." Below the eyebrow, three small blocks. *What's still here:* "Your protocol from April 3 is intact. Your habit stack still has magnesium glycinate, the iron-rich tier, and the afternoon walk." *What changed while you were away:* "Your ferritin projection window opened on April 22 and is still open — your next blood draw would land in the band we projected." *What's overdue:* "Your retest was scheduled for May 12. Push it to a date that works, or schedule the draw." At the bottom, two equally-sized links: "Pick up where I left off" → `#/today`. "Re-compose with the time off counted" → opens the Plan recompose flow with the gap pre-loaded as a known stretch of zero-adherence days. I tap pick up where I left off. I land on Today. The quiet-day card fires its normal precedence — but now with full context, because I've already absorbed the gap on the previous screen.

### Growth

This is the retention feature most precision-health apps cannot ship without weakening their privacy contract. The competing pattern — a server-side reactivation email — requires an email address, an email-sending pipeline, and the implicit user expectation that the app knows when they have stopped using it. Almanac is structurally barred from that pattern by the no-backend rule. The lapse-aware welcome-back surface is the local-first move that recovers the same retention benefit without any of the surveillance: it works *because* the user opened the app, not because we reached them. (Hypothesis: a lapse-aware reopen surface lifts the 60-day reactivation rate by 15–25% relative to dropping the user straight onto a stale Today screen; back-of-envelope from comparable patterns in Streaks-style habit apps that show a "missed days" summary on reopen — treat as untested until we have local-only telemetry to compare against a pre-0018 baseline.) The surface is also the first artifact that meaningfully demonstrates Almanac's *time* dimension. A friend who lapsed for a month, came back, and saw a single page tell them exactly what happened on the timeline while they were gone has the "this app remembers me" moment that converts a casual user into a long-term one.

## Acceptance criteria

Each box maps 1:1 to a Playwright test scenario.

- [ ] `src/db.ts` — add a `sessions` table (Dexie v7, additive over v6) with shape `{ id?: number; at: number }`. On every full-page load (in `src/main.ts` after the router resolves the first route), append a row with `at = Date.now()`. Helpers: `recordSession()`, `previousSessionAt(): number | null` (returns the most-recent row OLDER than the row recorded on this load — explicitly NOT the row recorded this load).
- [ ] `src/pages/welcome-back.ts` (new) — exports `renderWelcomeBack(state: WelcomeBackState): string` and `computeWelcomeBackState(now: number, prev: number | null, plan: Plan | null, panels: Panel[], projections: ProjectionSnapshot[], retest: RetestPlan | null): WelcomeBackState | null`. Returns `null` when (a) `prev` is null (first session ever), (b) the gap is less than 14 days, or (c) the user has no composed plan. Returns a populated state when the gap exceeds the threshold and a plan exists.
- [ ] `WelcomeBackState` carries: `daysAway: number`, `lastSessionAt: Day`, `whatsStill: { planComposedAt: Day; habitCount: number; habitTitles: string[] }`, `whatChanged: Array<{ kind: "projection-opened" | "projection-evaluated" | "recap-missed-count"; body: string; cta?: { label: string; href: string } }>`, `whatsOverdue: Array<{ kind: "retest-overdue" | "retest-coming-due"; body: string; cta: { label: string; href: string } }>`. Each list may be empty; the page renders the section heading only when its list is non-empty.
- [ ] On every full-page load when `isTour()` and `isSharedView()` are both false, the router checks if `computeWelcomeBackState()` would return non-null; when it would, AND the route is `#/today` (the default landing), the router redirects once to `#/welcome-back`. Other routes (`#/plan`, `#/labs`, etc.) do NOT trigger the redirect — a user who deep-links to a specific page lands on that page directly. The redirect happens at most once per session; the redirect sets a transient in-memory flag, so subsequent in-session navigations to `#/today` go to Today as expected.
- [ ] The welcome-back surface renders entirely from local data with **zero Anthropic calls**. Asserted across a full page load + render + both CTA paths via the mock's request count.
- [ ] `whatChanged` includes `projection-opened` rows for each `ProjectionSnapshot` whose `[weeksOut[0], weeksOut[1]]` window measured from `createdAt` opened while the user was away (`createdAt + weeksOut[0]*7d <= now AND createdAt + weeksOut[0]*7d > prev`). The body reads "Your <marker> projection window opened on <date> and is still open." with the CTA "Plan a retest" → `#/plan`.
- [ ] `whatChanged` includes `projection-evaluated` rows when a panel was uploaded during the gap that landed inside or outside an earlier projection band (per 0012's `evaluateLanded()` helper). The body reads "Your <marker> projection landed at <value> — <within|under|over> the projected band." This branch fires only when the panel was uploaded by an automated import (Apple Health, ticket 0004) since a non-engaged user cannot manually upload — confirm Apple Health import writes panels during the gap; if it does not, the branch returns no rows and the test for it asserts the empty case.
- [ ] `whatChanged` includes a single `recap-missed-count` row when at least one Sunday occurred during the gap. The body reads "<N> Sunday recap<s> happened while you were away. Tap to read the most recent." with the CTA "Read recap" → `#/recap`. Tested with gap lengths of 7, 21, and 42 days (one, three, and six missed Sundays respectively).
- [ ] `whatsOverdue` includes a `retest-overdue` row when `plan.retest.targetDate` exists AND is in the past relative to `now`. The body reads "Your retest was scheduled for <date>. <N> days ago." with the CTA "Update retest plan" → `#/plan`. A `retest-coming-due` row fires when the target date is within 14 days of `now` (future); body "Your retest is in <N> days." with the same CTA.
- [ ] `whatsStill.habitTitles` lists the active plan's habit titles verbatim from `plan.habitStack.habits`, capped at three (renders "and N more" tail when over three). This proves to the returning user that their protocol is intact without listing every micro-detail.
- [ ] The bottom of the page renders two CTAs of equal visual weight: "Pick up where I left off" (route to `#/today`, calls `recordSession()` is already done, no extra side effect) and "Re-compose with the time off counted" (route to `#/plan?recompose=lapse-aware` — a new query flag the Plan page reads to know it should populate the adherence summary with the gap as known zero-adherence days). The Plan page's existing compose flow gains a single new branch: when `recompose=lapse-aware` is set, `formatAdherence()` (in `src/claude.ts`) is fed the real `recentCheckIns(14)` PLUS a synthetic prefix of N skipped days where N is the gap duration in days (capped at 30). The synthetic days are not persisted; they exist only for the duration of the compose call.
- [ ] A small "Dismiss for today" link at the top-right of the welcome-back page sets `localStorage["almanac.welcomeBack.dismissed.<today-iso>"] = "true"` and routes to `#/today`. Same-day reopens skip the redirect. The next morning the welcome-back surface returns to its normal precedence (it fires again only if the gap criteria are still met — typically not, since the user just opened the app).
- [ ] Renders on both **chromium and mobile-webkit**. On mobile, the three sections stack; on desktop they render in a single column with generous left margin matching the recap page's layout from 0008.
- [ ] Schema migration: **yes** — Dexie v7 adds the `sessions` table. The migration test asserts a v6 export can be imported into a v7 build without loss; new `sessions` table starts empty for migrated users and begins populating on the first session after upgrade.
- [ ] Privacy E2E still passes — no new hostnames; the `sessions` table is local-only; no `at` timestamp leaves the device.
- [ ] New `tests/e2e/welcome-back.spec.ts` covers: first-session ever (no redirect — no `prev`), gap below threshold (no redirect), gap above threshold WITH no plan (no redirect — empty state hidden, not shown), gap above threshold WITH plan and projection-opened (redirect + section renders + CTA routes to plan), gap with retest-overdue (section renders + CTA routes to plan), gap of 21 days surfaces three missed recaps row, deep-link to `#/labs` during a lapse does NOT redirect, dismissed-for-today persists across in-session navigation, "Pick up where I left off" lands on Today and is the same Today the user would have seen without the redirect, "Re-compose with the time off counted" lands on Plan with the `recompose=lapse-aware` flag wired through the compose prompt (assertable via the mock's last prompt body — the synthetic skip days appear in the adherence block).

## Out of scope

Explicit anti-goals. The dev agent will not do these even if they seem related.

- A push notification or email reminder for inactive users. No notifications API, no backend. The whole point is that the surface only exists when the user themselves reopens the app — surveillance-free re-engagement is the design constraint that makes this novel.
- A streak-style "you broke your 17-day streak" guilt-trip header. The surface is editorial and neutral, not gamified. The gap is named factually ("It has been 42 days") and the catch-up CTAs are equally-weighted; there is no "you should feel bad" copy.
- A "what did you do during the lapse" survey (mood logging, weight changes, etc.). The surface uses only local data we already have; asking the user to retroactively log absent data is busywork on a page whose entire job is to reduce friction.
- LLM-generated welcome-back copy. The five sections are deterministic — the editorial voice is in the curated phrasing of the section headers and the per-row body templates, not in a Claude call. A second Claude call on the most-fragile moment of the funnel (a lapsed user's first reopen) is exactly the wrong place to introduce a network dependency.
- A "delete my data and start over" CTA. That belongs on `#/settings` (where it already lives). Surfacing it on the lapse-recovery page is a footgun.
- Lapse-aware behavior in tour mode (0014) or shared-view mode (0017). Both modes are session-scoped and have no persistent timeline; the welcome-back surface does not apply.
- A weekly or monthly history of past welcome-back surfaces. The page is a render-time view of current state; it is not persisted.
- A multi-account / household variant ("welcome back, here's what changed for everyone in your house"). Almanac is single-tenant; the household field in the profile is informational, not multi-user.

## Engineering notes

Files / patterns the dev should touch. Be specific enough that the dev doesn't have to re-discover the architecture.

- `src/db.ts` — add the `sessions` table at Dexie v7. The migration pattern mirrors how 0002 (v5) and 0012 (v6) added their tables: append a `.version(7).stores({ sessions: "++id, at" })` block, never edit prior version blocks. Helpers: `recordSession(): Promise<void>`, `previousSessionAt(currentSessionId: number): Promise<number | null>` (returns the `at` of the row whose id is largest and less than `currentSessionId`).
- `src/main.ts` — at the top of the post-route-resolved continuation, call `recordSession()` (await the id), then call `previousSessionAt(thisId)` to get the gap timestamp. Compute the `WelcomeBackState`; if non-null AND the resolved route is `#/today` AND `localStorage["almanac.welcomeBack.dismissed.<today-iso>"]` is falsy AND the in-memory once-per-session flag is unset, set the flag and redirect to `#/welcome-back`. Otherwise proceed normally. Both tour mode and shared-view mode short-circuit this entire block (they have their own routing branches that already win).
- `src/pages/welcome-back.ts` (new) — the page module per the acceptance bullets. Exports `renderWelcomeBack(state)` and `computeWelcomeBackState(now, prev, plan, panels, projections, retest)`. The computation is pure; the renderer paints. Both are unit-testable through the Playwright spec by driving the Dexie state and asserting DOM.
- `src/types.ts` — add `WelcomeBackState` and `WelcomeBackRow` shapes. All additive.
- `src/chrome.ts` — the masthead nav does not get a new "Welcome back" entry (the page is not a destination, it is a re-entry interstitial). The page renders the masthead minus any active-route indicator; it reads as a clean transition surface.
- `src/styles.css` — re-use the existing `.recap-card` / `.print-sheet` typography tokens. One new block `.welcome-back` for the three-section layout; no new color tokens. The section headings ("What is still here", "What changed while you were away", "What is overdue") render in the same small-caps eyebrow style as the recap sections.
- `src/pages/plan.ts` — read `URLSearchParams` for `recompose=lapse-aware`. When set, pass a `lapseAwareGap: number` (days) into the existing compose helper. The compose helper threads it into `formatAdherence()` in `src/claude.ts`.
- `src/claude.ts` — `formatAdherence(checkins, opts?)` gains an optional `opts.syntheticSkipDays?: number`. When set, the formatter prepends N synthetic skipped days to the adherence summary it produces for the prompt, labelled "User was away (no check-ins logged): N days." The synthetic days are NOT persisted; they live only inside the prompt for the lapse-aware compose call. The voice spec is honest: the prompt explicitly names the absence as a known unknown, not as zero adherence.
- `tests/helpers/flows.ts` — add `seedSessionGap(page, daysAgo: number)` and `clearSessions(page)` helpers. The seed helper writes a single `sessions` row with `at = Date.now() - daysAgo * 86_400_000` via `page.evaluate` against Dexie so the spec can drive each gap deterministically.
- `tests/e2e/welcome-back.spec.ts` (new) — every acceptance bullet maps to a `test()`. Re-uses `composePlan`, `addManualPanel`, and the seed helpers above. The "re-compose with the time off counted" test inspects `mocks.lastPromptBody()` after the recompose to confirm the synthetic skip-days line is present in the adherence block.
- `tests/e2e/privacy.spec.ts` — extend with one assertion that the `sessions` table exists and rows have only the `id` + `at` shape (no IP, no user-agent, no per-page tracking). The privacy contract is that the table is the minimum needed to power the local-only lapse detection.
- Schema migration: **yes** — Dexie v7 adds `sessions`. Migration test asserts no data loss for existing v6 users.
- Egress allow-list change: **no**.
- New deps: **no**.
- Voice spec for the welcome-back copy: editorial, neutral, factual. The eyebrow reads "Welcome back." (period, not exclamation). The gap line reads "It has been <N> days." No "we missed you," no "amazing to see you again," no journey language. Section headings are small caps. CTA labels are imperative and concrete ("Pick up where I left off", "Re-compose with the time off counted", "Read recap", "Update retest plan", "Plan a retest").
