## User story

As a user who is 100 days past my first composed plan and has logged at least 50 check-ins across that span, I want a single editorial surface — `#/hundred-day` — that fires once per quarter, names the actual arc of my protocol over those 100 days (which habits held, which slipped, which markers I tested and what they did, which insights stayed live across recomposes), reads like a printed almanac's seasonal page rather than a dashboard, and gives me one honest paragraph of what the next 100 days could reasonably hold given what the prior 100 actually did — so that the long-arc relationship I have with the protocol becomes visible to me, instead of being silently flattened into a daily streak strip and a weekly recap that never see each other.

## Why now (four lenses)

### Product Owner

Almanac has shipped four cadence surfaces — Today (daily), Quiet-day card (Mon–Sat, 0015), Sunday recap (weekly, 0008), and now the lapse-aware welcome-back (0018, between-sessions). None of them surface the *long arc*. The user who has been on a protocol for 100 days — a real quarterly check-in by every clinical convention — has no surface that says "here is what 100 days of you actually looks like." The closest is `#/progress`, which shows marker trends panel-by-panel, but Progress is per-marker not per-arc; it does not connect "you logged magnesium glycinate 73 of 100 days" to "your fasting insulin went from 12 to 9" to "the iron-restricted erythropoiesis pattern stopped firing after your April draw." The smallest meaningful unit of value is one editorial page that fires once when the user reaches day 100 from their first plan composition, renders deterministically from local data (no LLM call), and reads as a printed almanac's seasonal page — three sections: the arc (which habits held and at what rate), the labs (which markers moved and by how much, with the projection-evaluation hook from 0012 woven in), and the editorial paragraph (one honest sentence per moved-or-held finding, no claims beyond what the data shows). The surface lands again at day 200, day 300, and day 400, each one diffing the prior hundred from the one before it.

### Stakeholder

This is the **persistent timeline** moat finally getting the surface it has always deserved. The timeline has been load-bearing since v1 (every plan compose reads adherence; every recap reads check-ins; every projection reads panels). But the user has never *seen* the timeline at the scale it was built for. The weekly recap is a one-week snapshot; the projection cards are panel-to-panel comparisons. Nothing surfaces the entire quarterly arc as a single artifact. A backend-shaped product would ship this as a server-rendered "your year in <product>" recap email — Spotify Wrapped's mechanic. Almanac's local-first architecture lets us ship something fundamentally different: the recap is computed on-device from the actual timeline the user has been building, with zero data transfer, and it fires on the user's *real anniversary* (day 100 from their actual first plan, not a calendar anniversary chosen for marketing reasons). It deepens the moat by turning a structurally-invisible asset (the multi-month adherence + marker + insight timeline) into a *legible editorial artifact*. It also opens a clinical wedge: the hundred-day surface is exactly the artifact a functional-medicine practitioner would want to read at the user's quarterly follow-up — a single page of "what the protocol actually did," in the editorial voice, with marker-level evidence.

### User (at 7am on the phone)

Day 100 since my first plan. I open the app. The masthead nav shows the usual five entries, but a single small editorial card sits above Today's meals: "A quarterly note · 100 days." I tap it. The page renders. The eyebrow reads "The first hundred." The headline: "What 100 days actually did." Below, three sections in the editorial voice. *The arc:* "Magnesium glycinate held 73 of 100 days — strongest cadence. The afternoon walk held 41 of 100 — slipped from week 6 onward. The morning protein you added on day 14 held 81 of 86 days since." *The labs:* "You drew twice — March 12 and May 8. Ferritin moved from 32 to 48 ng/mL — inside the projection band we showed you on March 14. ApoB held steady at 91 mg/dL. Fasting insulin moved from 12.4 to 9.1 µIU/mL — outside the projected window, faster than we'd seen in the literature." *The editorial:* "The iron habit moved the iron biology. The cardiovascular habit (the walk) slipped, and ApoB did not move; that is not coincidence. Next quarter: hold the walk for two weeks before you change anything else." The page ends with a single CTA: "Read this as a one-page PDF" (re-uses the 0010 printable plumbing). I tap it. The PDF renders. I AirDrop it to my doctor.

### Growth

This is the retention ticket of the four, but it produces an unusually dense organic-growth artifact. The hundred-day page is the kind of thing a user screenshots and posts to r/PeterAttia or QuantifiedSelf — not because Almanac asked them to, but because the artifact itself reads as worth sharing. (Hypothesis: a hundred-day surface shipped against a real user's timeline produces ~5-10x the organic share rate of a single weekly recap, because the multi-month arc is the unit a precision-health audience actually argues about — single-week deltas are noise, quarterly arcs are signal; back-of-envelope from how Strava's annual recap converts to public posts vs Strava's weekly summary — treat as untested.) It also compounds 0017's share link: a user who runs the hundred-day surface and decides to share their protocol *now* is showing the friend three months of evidence, not just a snapshot. The acquisition value compounds with every shipped retention surface. And the hundred-day page is the first artifact whose mere existence is a competitive moat — no other wellness app has the structural prerequisites (a persistent on-device timeline + a deterministic insight engine that ran across that timeline + a marker-projection system + an adherence loop) to compose this page locally; backend-shaped competitors either don't have the data continuity or can't afford the per-user compute to render it server-side at scale.

## Acceptance criteria

Each box maps 1:1 to a Playwright test scenario.

- [ ] `src/progress/hundred-day.ts` (new) exports `computeHundredDayState(now: number, firstPlanGeneratedAt: number, allPlans: Plan[], allPanels: Panel[], allCheckIns: CheckIn[], allProjections: ProjectionSnapshot[]): HundredDayState | null`. Returns `null` when (a) no plan has been composed, OR (b) `now - firstPlanGeneratedAt < 100 days`, OR (c) the user has logged fewer than 50 check-ins across the 100-day window. Returns a populated state otherwise.
- [ ] `HundredDayState` carries: `quarterIndex: 1 | 2 | 3 | 4 | ...` (which 100-day block this is — 1 = days 1–100, 2 = 101–200, etc.), `windowStart: Day`, `windowEnd: Day`, `arc: ArcSection`, `labs: LabsSection`, `editorial: EditorialSection`. The function is pure (no DOM, no Dexie, no network) — asserted by importing and calling from a `page.evaluate` context with synthetic inputs and snapshotting the output.
- [ ] `ArcSection` shape: `habits: Array<{ id: string; title: string; heldDays: number; slipWeek?: number; addedOnDay?: number }>` — one row per habit that appeared in *any* plan during the window. `heldDays` is days the habit id appears in `CheckIn.habitsCompleted` within the window. `slipWeek` is the first week (1–14) where adherence dropped below 50% for that habit AND stayed below for the next two weeks (rolling threshold; null when the habit held). `addedOnDay` is the day-offset within the window when the habit first appeared in a recomposed plan (null when present from day 1).
- [ ] `LabsSection` shape: `panels: Array<{ drawnAt: Day; markerCount: number }>`, `markerMovements: Array<{ markerKey: string; firstValue: number; lastValue: number; unit: string; projectionEvaluation?: "within" | "above" | "below" | null }>`. `markerMovements` lists every marker that has at least two draws in the window, ordered by absolute delta descending. The projection evaluation re-uses `evaluateLanded()` from 0012 — for each marker that had a projection snapshot from a within-window panel and a follow-up panel that landed, report whether the actual value landed inside, above, or below the projected band. Otherwise null.
- [ ] `EditorialSection` shape: `paragraphs: Array<{ kind: "habit-marker-link" | "habit-slip" | "habit-add" | "marker-mover" | "marker-held"; sentence: string }>`. Each paragraph is one declarative sentence in the editorial voice. The composition is deterministic — a curated set of sentence templates fired off the structured ArcSection + LabsSection. Banned words from AGENTS.md ("journey", "amazing", "exciting", "great") are absent by construction.
- [ ] The page renders the three sections in order — arc, labs, editorial — under a single eyebrow ("The first hundred" for quarter 1; "The second hundred" for quarter 2; etc., continuing the editorial register). The headline reads "What <N> days actually did." where N is `windowEnd - windowStart + 1` (always 100 unless the function is extended). Section headings use the existing small-caps eyebrow tokens from the recap page (0008).
- [ ] On every full-page load when the user is past day 100 from their first plan AND has not dismissed the surface for this quarter, the Today screen surfaces a single small card above the meals row: "A quarterly note · <N> days". Tapping the card routes to `#/hundred-day`. The card uses the same `.recap-card` token from 0008 (restraint over novelty) with a `--quarterly` modifier for the eyebrow text.
- [ ] The card's "Not this quarter" dismissal stores `localStorage["almanac.hundredDay.dismissed.q<index>"] = "true"`. Dismissal is per-quarter — a user who dismissed quarter 1 still sees the card on day 200 for quarter 2.
- [ ] The hundred-day surface runs with **zero Anthropic calls**. Asserted across a full page load + render + the print CTA via the mock's request count.
- [ ] The "Read this as a one-page PDF" CTA re-uses `mountAndPrint()` from `src/print/protocol.ts` (the 0010 plumbing) with a new `Audience` value `"hundred-day"` or with a dedicated `mountAndPrintHundredDay(state)` shim that lays out the same three sections on a print sheet. The PDF audience is "user / clinician" (not "doctor" — the hundred-day is informational, not a clinical hand-off); the print template re-uses the existing `.print-sheet` typography tokens.
- [ ] On both **chromium and mobile-webkit**, the page renders. Mobile stacks the three sections; desktop renders them in a single column with the recap page's left margin. Touch targets on the dismissal and the print CTA are ≥44×44 css px.
- [ ] When `allPlans.length === 1` (the user never recomposed during the quarter), the `arc.habits` list reads from the single plan's habits. When `allPlans.length > 1` (the user recomposed mid-quarter), the arc unions the habits across plans, marking the `addedOnDay` for habits introduced mid-quarter. Tested with a fixture that has two recomposes inside the 100-day window.
- [ ] Markers with only one draw in the window do NOT appear in `markerMovements` (no delta is computable). The section heading still renders; the row reads "Two draws within the window are needed for a delta. You drew once — <date>." when `panels.length === 1`. When `panels.length === 0`, the labs section renders the empty state "No draws inside the window. Habit cadence is the only signal we have for this quarter."
- [ ] The editorial section's `habit-marker-link` paragraphs fire ONLY when both sides are statistically honest: a habit must have held at least 50% of the window AND a marker that habit targets (via `Habit.markerKeys`-style linkage if available, otherwise via the plan's `EatItem.markerKeys` for food-driven habits) must have moved by at least the marker's `responsiveness.magnitude.low` value. The link sentence is declarative ("The iron habit moved the iron biology.") not causal ("caused"). When no link qualifies, the paragraph is omitted; the section never invents.
- [ ] Privacy E2E (`tests/e2e/privacy.spec.ts`) still passes — no new hostnames; the hundred-day computation reads only IndexedDB; the PDF render uses the same on-device print path as 0010.
- [ ] Schema migration: **no**. The hundred-day surface is computed on every render from existing tables (`plans`, `panels`, `checkins`, `projections`); no new persistent state beyond the per-quarter dismissal localStorage key.
- [ ] Tour mode (0014) and shared-view mode (0017) do NOT trigger the hundred-day card or route. Both modes are session-scoped and have no real timeline; the surface short-circuits at the `isTour() || isSharedView()` check. Asserted by entering the tour and confirming the Today card and `#/hundred-day` route are both absent.
- [ ] New `tests/e2e/hundred-day.spec.ts` covers: pre-day-100 user does not see the card or route, day-100 user with 50+ check-ins sees the card, dismissal sticks for the quarter and clears on quarter 2, arc section lists habits with hit counts, labs section lists marker movements ordered by absolute delta, projection evaluation surfaces "within"/"above"/"below" per 0012's `evaluateLanded()`, editorial section omits unjustified paragraphs (a user with no marker movements gets no `marker-mover` paragraph), the PDF print path renders all three sections, zero-network assertion across a full render, tour mode does not surface the card.

## Out of scope

Explicit anti-goals. The dev agent will not do these even if they seem related.

- An LLM-generated narrative paragraph. The editorial sentences are deterministic templates fired off structured data; introducing a Claude call here would (a) re-introduce non-determinism on the user's quarterly artifact, (b) tempt the rendering to claim things the data doesn't support, and (c) add cost / latency on a surface that should open in under 200ms.
- A "send my hundred-day to my doctor" email path. The PDF + native share sheet (already wired by 0010) is the entire share surface. Adding an email path requires a backend.
- A confetti / streak / "you crushed it" celebration overlay. The editorial register is honest, not congratulatory. A habit that held 41 of 100 days reads as "slipped from week 6 onward" — declarative, not punitive, not celebratory.
- A multi-quarter combined view ("the first 300 days"). Each quarter renders as its own surface; cross-quarter combinators are a future ticket if the value emerges with real users.
- A per-marker projection FOR the next 100 days. Projection (0012) is panel-to-panel; the hundred-day editorial paragraph's "next quarter" line is observational ("hold the walk for two weeks before you change anything else"), not predictive of marker values.
- A push notification when day 100 arrives. No notification API, no backend. The Today card fires when the user opens the app on or after day 100; that is the entire reminder mechanism.
- Custom-window quarters (60-day, 120-day). The 100-day window is the editorial unit; allowing the user to slide it would dilute the artifact. If a future ticket establishes a different cadence (semi-annual, annual), it ships as its own surface.
- A "share this hundred-day" PNG mirroring the marker hero card (0011). The hundred-day is a multi-section page; a single PNG would lose the structure. The PDF is the share artifact; users can screenshot a section if they want a tile.
- Surfacing the hundred-day inside `#/recap` or `#/progress`. The hundred-day is its own route, with its own dismissal cadence; folding it into another surface would muddy both.

## Engineering notes

Files / patterns the dev should touch. Be specific enough that the dev doesn't have to re-discover the architecture.

- `src/progress/hundred-day.ts` (new) — `computeHundredDayState()` per the acceptance bullets. Pure function; the page passes in Plans / Panels / CheckIns / Projections from the read shims. The function lives under `src/progress/` so it sits alongside `compare.ts` (0009) and `projection.ts` (0012), the existing per-arc computations. Re-uses `evaluateLanded()` from `src/progress/projection.ts` for the labs section.
- `src/pages/hundred-day.ts` (new) — the page module. `renderHundredDay()` reads the latest plan + all panels + recent check-ins (the full window — 100 days of check-ins, not the recap's 14) + projections, calls `computeHundredDayState()`, and paints. The page registers in `src/main.ts`'s router under the path `#/hundred-day`. No new masthead nav entry — the page is reached via the Today card or by direct URL.
- `src/main.ts` — register `#/hundred-day` in `dispatch()`. The route follows the same gate behavior as `#/recap` — requires consent and a profile; does NOT bypass either; tour and shared-view mode short-circuit.
- `src/pages/today.ts` — at the top of the render function (above the existing recap-card / quiet-card chain), call a new `quarterlyCardActive(profile, plans)` helper that returns true when the user is past day 100 from their first plan AND has 50+ check-ins AND has not dismissed the current quarter. Render the small editorial card with the "A quarterly note · <N> days" eyebrow when active. The card stacks above the Sunday recap card and the quiet-day card; precedence is quarterly > recap (Sunday) > quiet-day (Mon–Sat). Each card is mutually exclusive — only the highest-precedence one renders.
- `src/db.ts` — add `allCheckInsInWindow(startDay: Day, endDay: Day): Promise<CheckIn[]>` and `firstPlanGeneratedAt(): Promise<number | null>` (returns the `generatedAt` of the oldest plan; null when none exist). Both are simple Dexie reads.
- `src/print/protocol.ts` — extend the existing `mountAndPrint()` signature with a new audience case, OR add a parallel `mountAndPrintHundredDay(state: HundredDayState): Promise<void>` that mounts a print template specific to the hundred-day. The print template re-uses `.print-sheet` typography tokens; no new colors.
- `src/print/template.ts` — add a `renderHundredDayTemplate(state: HundredDayState): string` exporting the print-sheet HTML for the three sections. The template re-uses the same drop-cap / small-caps / section-mark patterns as the existing 0010 protocol-one-pager.
- `src/types.ts` — add `HundredDayState`, `ArcSection`, `LabsSection`, `EditorialSection`, `ArcHabit`, `MarkerMovement`, `EditorialParagraph`. All additive.
- `src/chrome.ts` — no changes; the hundred-day is reached via the Today card or direct URL.
- `src/styles.css` — `.hundred-day` block (three-section layout, mirrors the recap page from 0008), `.hundred-day__arc-row` (one row per habit), `.hundred-day__movement-row` (one row per marker). The "A quarterly note" card on Today re-uses `.recap-card` with a `.recap-card--quarterly` modifier for the eyebrow accent.
- `tests/helpers/flows.ts` — add `seedHundredDayState(page, opts)` that seeds the IndexedDB with a synthetic plan (generated `opts.daysAgo` days ago), N check-ins spanning the window, and optional panels + projections. Used by the spec to drive every code path without re-running the full compose flow.
- `tests/e2e/hundred-day.spec.ts` (new) — every acceptance bullet maps to a `test()` or `expect()`. The deterministic-editorial assertion seeds a known state and confirms the rendered editorial paragraphs match an expected list of sentences (no substring fuzzing — the templates are deterministic, the assertion is exact).
- `tests/e2e/privacy.spec.ts` — extend the existing sweep with a `#/hundred-day` visit to confirm the route renders with no off-allow-list egress.
- Schema migration: **no**.
- Egress allow-list change: **no**.
- New deps: **no**.
- Voice spec for the editorial section: declarative, no claims beyond what the data shows, banned-word-free. The "next quarter" suggestion is observational ("hold the walk for two weeks before you change anything else") not predictive ("you'll see X by August"). Each paragraph is one sentence. Banned: "amazing," "exciting," "journey," "great," "crushed," "smashed," any congratulatory register. The eyebrow is "The first hundred" / "The second hundred" / "The third hundred" — Roman ordinals through six, then "Quarter N" past that.
