## User story

As a user reading my Plan after a recompose, I want each insight card to expose three small actions — pin (I am actively working this), snooze for 30 days (I have read this and want it to stop surfacing), dismiss with a one-line reason ("addressed by my doctor on May 8") — so that my stance on each insight becomes part of the persistent timeline, my Plan and Today pages reflect the stances I have taken (pinned insights surface at the top; snoozed ones collapse under a small "3 snoozed" disclosure; dismissed ones drop out entirely but live on in a "previously dismissed" log on Progress), and the next plan compose reads the stances back into the prompt as adherence-equivalent signal — so that the engine learns I have an opinion, and the protocol becomes mine in a way a single compose cannot achieve.

## Why now (four lenses)

### Product Owner

The Plan page today renders the same insight cards every time — there is no relationship between the user and an individual insight beyond the implicit one of "you saw it." A user three weeks into a protocol may have actively addressed a finding ("my doctor checked my B12 and we are not supplementing"), or may have decided to let it sit ("I will revisit ApoB after the next draw"), or may have committed to it ("the iron-restricted pattern is what I am working on this quarter"). None of those stances are first-class. The smallest meaningful unit of value is one row of three small affordances on every insight card — pin, snooze, dismiss — with the side effect of writing a single `InsightStance` row to a new Dexie table and re-rendering the Plan / Today / Progress pages against the stance set. The stance is the user's voice on the engine's output; capturing it is the first move toward a protocol that is mutually authored rather than one-way prescribed.

### Stakeholder

This is the **adherence loop** moat extending into a new dimension: not "did you do the habit" but "what is your stance on the finding." The adherence loop has been load-bearing for plan composition (since v1) on the habit side; the marker side has been read-only — the engine emits findings, the user absorbs them, and the next compose reads adherence on habits but not on insights. Insight stance closes that loop. A pinned insight tells the next compose: "the user wants this finding to drive their protocol — weight the habit stack toward this." A snoozed insight tells the next compose: "the user has read this and prefers it not surface for 30 days — surface other findings if any." A dismissed insight with a free-form reason tells the next compose: "the user has addressed this externally — do not surface it; reference the reason in the snapshot." A backend-shaped product would ship this as a server-mediated feedback loop; Almanac's local-first architecture lets us ship it as a single Dexie table read by the next prompt, with the user's reasons living in their device. It also deepens the persistent-timeline moat: the stance log on `#/progress` is the first surface where the user can see their own relationship to the engine evolve over months.

### User (at 7am on the phone)

Tuesday morning. I open `#/plan`. The iron-restricted erythropoiesis card is at the top. I tap the small "Pin" pill on its bottom row. The card slides up to the "Pinned" section header that just appeared. The Sunday recap card (when it surfaces) starts to surface the pinned insight's habit linkage — "you are pinning iron-restricted erythropoiesis. The iron-rich tier held 12 of 14 days this week." Down the page, the "elevated fasting insulin pattern" card sits with three small actions. I tap "Snooze 30 days." The card folds into a small "1 snoozed" disclosure at the bottom of the insights section. It will not surface again until June 18. Further down, "B12 below functional floor." I tap "Dismiss." A single-line input appears: "Reason (optional, one line)." I type "Addressed by my doctor on May 8." Enter. The card drops out of the list. On `#/progress` later I scroll to the "Previously dismissed" section. The B12 line is there with my reason and the date. Next month I recompose. The new plan's snapshot opens with a single sentence: "You have pinned iron-restricted erythropoiesis. You have dismissed B12 (addressed externally). The protocol below reflects both."

### Growth

This is the moat-deepening + retention ticket of the four — it does not produce a viral artifact, but it produces the structural commitment that turns a casual user into a long-term one. A user who has pinned three insights and snoozed two has invested specific opinions about their biology into the app; switching to a competitor means re-curating that stance set from scratch on a less-thoughtful surface. (Hypothesis: a user who has taken stance on at least one insight has roughly 2-3x the 90-day retention of a user who has only run compose; back-of-envelope from how Anki's "leech" mechanic and Roam Research's page-pin retention curves play out — treat as untested.) The second-order growth value is that pinned insights, surfaced on the Today page and on the Sunday recap, become the small daily anchor that re-anchors the user to *why* they are running the protocol — a re-acquisition mechanism that does not require a backend, a notification, or a server-mediated re-engagement campaign.

## Acceptance criteria

Each box maps 1:1 to a Playwright test scenario.

- [ ] `InsightStance` shape (in `src/types.ts`): `{ id?: number; insightFingerprint: string; stance: "pinned" | "snoozed" | "dismissed"; reason?: string; createdAt: number; snoozeUntil?: Day }`. `insightFingerprint` is a stable identifier for an insight across recomposes — for engine insights it is the rule id (`provenance.ruleId`); for LLM-only insights it is a SHA-256 of the `title` lowercased + trimmed (so an LLM that rewords the title slightly across composes does NOT lose its stance — the fingerprint is the title's essence).
- [ ] `src/insights/stance.ts` (new) exports `computeFingerprint(insight: Insight): string`, `listStances(): Promise<InsightStance[]>`, `setStance(fingerprint: string, stance: "pinned" | "snoozed" | "dismissed", reason?: string): Promise<void>`, `clearStance(fingerprint: string): Promise<void>`. The fingerprint helper is pure; the others read/write the new `insightStances` Dexie table.
- [ ] On every Plan page render, `listStances()` is called and the rendered insights are partitioned: pinned insights render at the TOP of the insights section under a "Pinned" eyebrow; the unmarked / un-snoozed-or-expired insights render in the middle in their original priority order; snoozed insights (with `snoozeUntil > today`) collapse under a single disclosure "<N> snoozed — show" that, when tapped, reveals them inline with a small "snoozed until <date>" footer; dismissed insights do not render on the Plan page at all.
- [ ] Each insight card gains a small three-action row beneath the existing "Why this fired" chip (from 0013): "Pin", "Snooze 30d", "Dismiss". Tapping "Pin" sets stance pinned and re-renders. Tapping "Snooze 30d" sets stance snoozed with `snoozeUntil = today + 30d` and re-renders. Tapping "Dismiss" surfaces an inline one-line reason input (free-form, ≤ 120 chars, optional); pressing Enter commits the dismiss with the reason. The actions are keyboard-focusable, use `role="button"`, and are styled as small-caps eyebrow links in ink (not oxblood — stance is informational, not a call-to-action).
- [ ] A pinned insight surfaces a small editorial line on `#/today` above the meals row, the day after it is pinned (the first render in a new local day): "You pinned <insight title>. <One sentence from `RULE_GLOSSES` or the insight's detail truncated to 120 chars>." The line is bounded by the existing recap/quiet-card precedence — pinned-insight line surfaces only when no higher-precedence card is active (quarterly > Sunday recap > quiet-day > pinned-insight line). Tapping the line routes to `#/plan` scrolled to the pinned card.
- [ ] A "Previously dismissed" section on `#/progress` lists every dismissed insight with its fingerprint, its title, the dismissal reason (or "(no reason)" when absent), and the dismissal date. The section renders only when at least one dismissal exists. Each row has a single "Re-surface" action that clears the stance and the insight reappears on the next Plan compose. The section uses the same `.recap-card` token from 0008 (restraint over novelty).
- [ ] On the next plan compose, `formatStances(stances)` (new helper in `src/claude.ts`) writes a single labeled block into the prompt summarizing the user's stances: "User stances on prior insights: pinned: <titles>; dismissed (addressed externally): <title — reason>; snoozed (do not surface): <title>." The LLM is instructed in `PLAN_VOICE` to honor the stances — pinned insights bias the habit stack and eat list toward addressing them; dismissed insights are not surfaced even if the rule would re-fire; snoozed insights are not surfaced until `snoozeUntil`. Empty stance set produces no block (the prompt is byte-identical to pre-0023).
- [ ] A dismissed insight whose rule re-fires on a subsequent compose does NOT re-surface — the rule output is filtered against the active stance set BEFORE the prompt is built. `computeInsights()` returns the full unfiltered list (the engine stays honest); the filtering happens in the `plan.ts` compose helper that translates engine output into the prompt's "deterministic findings" block. The filtering is asserted by seeding a dismissed B12 stance, recomposing, and confirming `mocks.lastPromptBody()` does NOT contain "B12 below functional floor" in the findings list.
- [ ] A snoozed insight whose `snoozeUntil` has passed automatically re-surfaces on the next render — no explicit "un-snooze" action is needed. The stance row remains in the table for history but the page treats it as if no stance is set. A user who wants to snooze again does so explicitly.
- [ ] User-authored rules (from 0022, once shipped) participate in stance the same way curated rules do — the fingerprint is the user rule's id (`"user:<uuid>"`), and pin / snooze / dismiss all work identically. Asserted by a spec that seeds a user rule, has it fire, dismisses it, and confirms the dismissal sticks across recompose.
- [ ] Tour mode (0014) and shared-view mode (0017) do NOT render stance actions. Both modes are session-scoped; capturing stance against fixture / payload data would muddy the persistent timeline. The actions are hidden when `isTour() || isSharedView()`.
- [ ] Export / import round-trips stances. The exported JSON carries an `insightStances: InsightStance[]` field. Import merges by `insightFingerprint` (newer `createdAt` wins on conflict). Round-trip asserted by export → wipe → import → re-render with the pinned section intact.
- [ ] Privacy E2E (`tests/e2e/privacy.spec.ts`) still passes — no new hostnames; stances are local-only; the prompt-side block fires only on compose and routes through the existing BYOK egress.
- [ ] Schema migration: **yes** — Dexie v(N+1) adds the `insightStances` table. Schema `++id, &insightFingerprint, stance, createdAt`. The unique index on `insightFingerprint` means a user cannot hold two stances on the same finding simultaneously — setting a new stance overwrites the old one.
- [ ] Renders on both **chromium and mobile-webkit**. On mobile, the three-action row stacks under the card; touch targets ≥44×44 css px.
- [ ] New `tests/e2e/insight-stance.spec.ts` covers: pin an insight, card moves to top under "Pinned"; snooze an insight, card collapses under disclosure; dismiss an insight with a reason, card disappears and surfaces on Progress's previously-dismissed section; dismissed insight does NOT re-surface in the next compose's prompt (assertable via `mocks.lastPromptBody()`); snoozed insight auto-expires after 30 days (assertable by faking `Date.now()` via the existing time-travel helper); pinned insight surfaces on Today the next day; pinned + dismissed + snoozed all round-trip through export / import; tour mode hides the actions; user-authored rule (when 0022 is shipped) participates in stance.

## Out of scope

Explicit anti-goals. The dev agent will not do these even if they seem related.

- A "snooze until a specific date" picker. 30 days is the editorial unit; allowing arbitrary snooze durations dilutes the surface and invites endless tinkering. A user who wants to revisit sooner clears the stance manually; a user who wants to revisit later snoozes a second time when the first 30-day window expires.
- A "remind me to revisit" notification when a snooze expires. No notification API. The snooze auto-expires silently; the next Plan render surfaces the insight.
- A "comment thread" on each insight (multi-row reasons over time). One row, one reason. A user who wants to update the reason re-dismisses the insight with a new reason; the prior dismissal is overwritten (the `&insightFingerprint` unique index enforces this).
- An LLM-generated "this is why we think you snoozed this" reflection. The reason is the user's; the engine does not infer it.
- Carrying stance into the shared protocol link (0017). The stance is the user's relationship with their own findings; sharing it would muddy the share artifact's privacy contract. Stances stay strictly local.
- Carrying stance into the doctor PDF (0010 / 0016). The doctor PDF is a structured snapshot of the clinical signal; surfacing the user's stances would dilute the clinical voice. A future ticket might add a "patient priorities" section if real demand emerges.
- Pinning more than five insights. The pinned section is bounded; a user attempting to pin a sixth sees an inline "You have five pinned. Unpin one to add another." message. Five is editorial; if more emerges as a real user need, the cap is the right place to revisit.
- A "bulk dismiss" affordance ("dismiss all low-priority"). Each stance is an individual editorial act; bulk operations would invite muscle-memory dismissal of insights the user did not actually consider.
- Reading stance into the Sunday recap (0008) or the hundred-day surface (0021, once shipped) beyond the existing read paths. The recap and the hundred-day are deterministic; surfacing stance in them is a future ticket if the value emerges.

## Engineering notes

Files / patterns the dev should touch. Be specific enough that the dev doesn't have to re-discover the architecture.

- `src/insights/stance.ts` (new) — `computeFingerprint(insight)`, `listStances()`, `setStance(fingerprint, stance, reason?)`, `clearStance(fingerprint)`, `formatStancesForPrompt(stances)`. The fingerprint helper prefers `provenance.ruleId` when present (engine insights); falls back to a SHA-256 of `insight.title.trim().toLowerCase()` (LLM-only insights). The hash uses `crypto.subtle.digest` — universally available.
- `src/types.ts` — add `InsightStance` and the literal stance union. All additive.
- `src/db.ts` — add the `insightStances` table at the next Dexie version. The schema `++id, &insightFingerprint, stance, createdAt`. The unique index on `insightFingerprint` enforces single-stance-per-finding.
- `src/pages/plan.ts` — re-render the insights section against the partitioned stance set. Each insight card gains the three-action row. The "Pinned" eyebrow renders only when at least one pinned insight is present. The snoozed disclosure renders only when at least one snoozed insight is present and not yet expired. Dismissed insights are filtered out entirely.
- `src/pages/today.ts` — at the top of the render chain (above the recap / quiet-card chain), add a `pinnedInsightLine(plan, stances, today)` helper. Returns the editorial line when a pinned insight exists AND no higher-precedence card is active. The function is pure; the page renders the line.
- `src/pages/progress.ts` — add the "Previously dismissed" section below the existing trend charts. Lists rows with title + reason + date + a "Re-surface" action. Renders only when ≥ 1 dismissal exists.
- `src/claude.ts` — `formatStancesForPrompt(stances)` writes the labeled block. `PLAN_VOICE` gains the one-paragraph instruction described in the acceptance bullets. The compose helper filters `computeInsights()` output against the stance set BEFORE building the deterministic-findings prompt block — `engineOutput.filter(i => !isDismissed(i, stances) && !isSnoozed(i, stances))`.
- `src/styles.css` — `.insight-stance-row` (the three-action row), `.insights--pinned` (the top section), `.insights__snoozed-disclosure` (the collapsing disclosure), `.insight-stance-reason-input` (the inline reason field). No new colors; ink-soft for the snoozed disclosure, oxblood reserved.
- `tests/helpers/flows.ts` — add `setInsightStance(page, fingerprint, stance, reason?)` and `clearInsightStances(page)` helpers via `page.evaluate` against Dexie. The time-travel helper (used by the existing 14-day-window adherence specs) is re-used to fake snooze expiry.
- `tests/e2e/insight-stance.spec.ts` (new) — every acceptance bullet maps to a test. The "dismissed insight does not re-surface in next compose" test seeds a panel that fires the iron-restricted rule, dismisses the resulting insight, recomposes, and asserts `mocks.lastPromptBody()` does not contain the rule's findings string.
- `tests/e2e/privacy.spec.ts` — extend with one assertion that the stance table is local-only and that a pinned-insight Today line does not fire any network request.
- Schema migration: **yes** — Dexie v(N+1) adds `insightStances`.
- Egress allow-list change: **no**.
- New deps: **no**.
- Voice spec: action labels are "Pin," "Snooze 30d," "Dismiss" — declarative imperatives, no exclamation. The reason input prompt is "Reason (optional, one line)." The Previously-dismissed row reads "<Title> — <reason> — <date>." or "<Title> — (no reason) — <date>." The pinned-Today line reads "You pinned <insight title>. <RULE_GLOSSES sentence or detail truncated>." Banned: "amazing," "exciting," "journey," "great." The compose-prompt block reads "User stances on prior insights: pinned: …; dismissed (addressed externally): …; snoozed (do not surface): …" — colon-delimited, declarative.
